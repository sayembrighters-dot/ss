Our Mission & Vision

Mission: "Empowering the youth & next generation to build a sustainable future for the people & planetary well-being." We are a youth-led nonprofit organization founded in 2016, dedicated to climate justice and environmental sustainability.

Focus Areas: Climate Justice & Just Transition, Climate Education, Youth Mobilization, Environmental Justice & Resilience. We work across 51 districts in Bangladesh with 20,000+ volunteers and 600+ Climate Action Ambassadors.

Partnerships: We collaborate with ActionAid Bangladesh, UNDP, Global Platform Bangladesh, and Bangladesh Youth COP to amplify our impact and reach.

What is Climate Justice?

Climate Justice is a concept that emphasizes the equitable distribution of the impacts of climate change. The main point is:

  • The countries most responsible for climate change (such as rich countries, which have historically emitted more greenhouse gases) must take the most responsibility.
  • Those who are least responsible for climate change (such as poor countries, low-income communities, indigenous people, young people and future generations) but are most affected must be given priority to solve them.
  • It is linked to human rights, equity and social justice – such as the rights to food, water, health and security.

Key Principles:

Procedural Justice: Including everyone (especially vulnerable groups) in decision-making.

Distributive Justice: Sharing costs and benefits fairly (e.g., providing climate finance to poor countries).

Historical Responsibility: Those who have polluted more in the past should pay more.

Just Transition: Providing fair support for workers and communities as they move from fossil fuels to renewable energy.

For the People and Planet

"Climate action is not just about saving the planet – it's about creating a world where people and nature thrive together."

What does "For the People and Planet" mean?

This slogan emphasizes the need to put the well-being of people and the planet together. It links climate action with sustainable development - balancing human rights, health, the economy and the environment while tackling climate change.

This theme is central to the UNEP (United Nations Environment Programme) strategy, which works towards climate stability, nature protection and a pollution-free planet.

Our Commitment: We believe that environmental protection and social justice are inseparable. Every climate solution must prioritize both planetary health and human dignity.

Our Key Programs & Initiatives

Climate Justice & Just Transition

Advocating for climate justice and supporting communities in transitioning to sustainable practices. Active participation in COP30 and international climate negotiations.

Climate Education

Developing climate education materials in Bengali, including the "Gohorotno" comic book distributed to 25+ schools across Bangladesh.

Youth Mobilization

Training and empowering young climate activists through fellowships, including our Climate Policy Negotiation Fellowship program.

Environmental Justice & Resilience

Building environmental resilience in communities and advocating for environmental justice at grassroots and policy levels.
